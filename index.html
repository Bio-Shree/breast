Clinical Decision Path

Each case is one hospital day. Baseline reads the prompt and gives one direct answer. Breast Cancer RL Environment adds the product tool layer: it organizes patient state, checks recent history, reviews action evidence, applies safety constraints, limits the answer to the allowed vocabulary, and then assigns reward against documented care.

1. InputThe hospital-day chart state enters the environment. 2. Product ToolsThe environment gathers context before the final action. 3. DecisionThe agent chooses one controlled care action. 4. RewardThe answer is scored against ground truth and guardrails.

Get patient details

get_case_brief summarizes the patient, cancer context, admission trajectory, and current hospital-day objective.

Case-level orientation.

Get action evidence

Evidence-to-action mapping.

Get temporal trends

get_temporal_trend_summary summarizes recent movement in symptoms, oxygen needs, labs, and treatment response.

Day-to-day trajectory.

Get safety constraints

get_safety_constraints surfaces critical-miss and contraindicated-action checks relevant to the selected hospital day.

Safety-aware selection control.

Get vitals

get_patient_vitals provides oxygen, blood pressure, temperature, and other bedside clinical status signals.

Clinical stability context signals.

Get labs

get_patient_labs provides renal, infectious, hematologic, and treatment-relevant laboratory signals for action selection.

Objective data context.

Get diagnoses

Get visit history

Within-visit memory context.

Get prior visits

Get guidelines

Tasks

Task Groups

The benchmark contains 22 inpatient-day tasks grouped across three breast-cancer hospitalization phases.

Visit 1: Initial admission

Early trajectory: diagnostic uncertainty, infection risk, pleural effusion, antibiotics, diuresis, and discharge planning.

  • T01Admission workup and pleural concern
  • T02Diagnostics vs antibiotic continuation
  • T03Neutropenic fever context
  • T04Ongoing infection management
  • T05Effusion and procedure ambiguity
  • T06Antibiotics vs diuretic adjustment
  • T07Pre-discharge antibiotic choice
  • T08Discharge-linked action day

Visit 2: Recurrent effusion

Middle trajectory: recurrent pleural disease, thoracentesis, chest tube or pleurodesis path, infection treatment, and discharge decisions.

  • T09Admission workup vs pleural procedure
  • T10Thoracentesis signal
  • T12Pleural drainage escalation
  • T13Infection plus effusion
  • T14Persistent effusion management
  • T15Chest tube or pleurodesis action
  • T16Discharge with clinical order

Visit 3: Advanced disease

Late trajectory: respiratory support, diagnostic preservation, palliative consult, goals-of-care change, hospice transition, and discharge.

  • T17Acute oxygen or BiPAP support
  • T18Hypoxia management without premature hospice
  • T19Palliative support vs formal transition
  • T20Palliative consult and family meeting
  • T22Hospice discharge planning
13-Action Vocabulary

The environment constrains output to these action labels; each label has a concise operational meaning for auditability.

Reward Functions
exact_primary

Strict primary match

Scores 1.0 only when the predicted primary action equals the hidden primary action.

Baseline 27.3% (6/22); environment 59.1% (13/22).

co_primary_exact

Accepted primary set

Scores credit if the prediction matches any accepted co-primary action.

Baseline 45.5% (10/22); environment 72.7% (16/22).

primary_or_secondary_match

Partial action credit

Gives credit for strict primary, co-primary, and documented secondary actions.

Baseline 0.500; environment 0.8295.

weighted_action_score_reward

Gold action support

Rewards the model in proportion to gold action support for its predicted action.

Baseline 0.511; environment 0.8518.
